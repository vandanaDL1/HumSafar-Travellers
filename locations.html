Discover The Beautiful World

Kashmir

Heaven on Earth Kashmir is one of the most beautiful travel destinations to visit in North India. Nowhere in India you will behold the scenic landscapes, icy glaciers, pristine lakes and lofty mountains as beautiful as Kashmir. Serenity and Tranquility redefines itself from the ambiance of Kashmir. Be it summer, Winter or Monsoon, every season has its own charm in Kashmir. The numerous wonders of nature, culture, cuisines and rich history of Kashmir will elevate your travel experience in Incredible India.

India(Agra)

The Taj Mahal, situated in Agra, India, is an iconic symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is renowned for its exquisite white marble architecture and intricate craftsmanship. The mausoleum's majestic dome, flanked by four minarets, creates a mesmerizing silhouette against the sky. Intricate marble inlay work, geometric patterns, and calligraphy adorn its walls and interiors. Set amidst lush gardens and reflecting pools, the Taj Mahal captivates millions of visitors annually, offering a timeless tribute to eternal love and architectural splendor.

Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, India, is a mesmerizing testament to the Vijayanagara Empire's grandeur. Its sprawling ruins, set amidst stunning boulder-strewn landscapes along the Tungabhadra River, evoke a bygone era of opulence and architectural brilliance. Hampi's iconic Virupaksha Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and the majestic Vittala Temple, known for its intricate stone chariot and musical pillars, are architectural marvels. Visitors explore the royal quarters, vibrant markets, and the imposing elephant stables. Hampi's rich history, depicted through its ruins and rock-cut monuments, coupled with its surreal natural beauty, attracts history enthusiasts, photographers, and spiritual seekers alike.

Statue Of Unity

The Statue of Unity, located in Gujarat, India, is the world's tallest statue, standing at an impressive height of 182 meters (597 feet). Dedicated to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, a key figure in India's independence movement and the country's first Deputy Prime Minister, the statue symbolizes unity and national pride. Designed by renowned sculptor Ram V. Sutar, it showcases Patel's towering presence and leadership. Visitors can explore the museum at the statue's base, which narrates Patel's life and contributions to India's unity. Offering panoramic views of the Narmada River and surrounding landscapes, the Statue of Unity has become a prominent landmark attracting tourists and patriots alike.

Isha Yoga

Shiva Coimbatore, also known as "Shiva of Coimbatore," is a revered deity located in the city of Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu, India. The towering statue stands at a height of 34 meters (112 feet) and depicts Lord Shiva in a majestic meditative pose, known as the "Adiyogi" or the "first yogi." It is recognized as the largest bust sculpture of Lord Shiva in the world. The statue serves as a symbol of spirituality, inner peace, and the profound yogic tradition that originated in India. Visitors flock to this iconic landmark to marvel at its grandeur and seek spiritual solace amidst the bustling city life.

Goa

Goa, renowned for its pristine beaches, is a coastal paradise on the western coast of India. With over 100 kilometers of coastline, it offers a diverse range of beaches catering to every taste, from bustling party hubs to secluded coves. Visitors flock to iconic beaches like Calangute, Baga, and Anjuna for vibrant nightlife and water sports. Palolem and Agonda entice with their serene ambiance and palm-fringed shores, ideal for relaxation. The beaches of Goa are adorned with golden sands, turquoise waters, and lined with shacks serving delicious seafood and refreshing drinks, making them a haven for sun-seekers and beach enthusiasts year-round.

Port Blair

Port Blair is an alluring destination for tourists as it includes a host of attractive locales. There are shimmering but clean beaches, beaches that defy the ferocity of sea and let tourists swim leisurely, relics of colonial power and oppression, many must-go museums, libraries, coral reefs, and more.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism, located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. Its glistening golden façade and sacred pool, the Amrit Sarovar, draw millions of pilgrims and visitors annually. The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das Ji, the fourth Sikh Guru, and completed by Guru Arjan Dev Ji in the 16th century. The shrine symbolizes equality, unity, and service, welcoming people from all walks of life regardless of religion or background. Visitors partake in the langar, a free communal meal, and experience the spiritual serenity of the temple's sanctum, embodying the Sikh principles of humility and compassion.
